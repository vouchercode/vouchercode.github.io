## Understanding the Core Changes: Asset Segregation and Proof-of-Reserve 2.0

The 2026 SEC rules fundamentally alter how exchanges like Binance must custody user assets. The key change is the mandatory **Real-Time Segregated Custody (RTSC)** model.

*   **What it Means for You**: A larger portion of user assets must be held in designated, auditable cold and warm wallets, distinct from the exchange's operational funds. This enhances security but may slightly affect the speed of processing very large withdrawals during extreme volatility, as pre-approved quotas are managed.
*   **Proof-of-Reserve (PoR) 2.0**: Binance is now required to provide daily, auditor-attested PoR reports that specifically map to the new segregated wallets. As a user, you should regularly check these reports via the Binance Transparency page to verify the security of the ecosystem.
