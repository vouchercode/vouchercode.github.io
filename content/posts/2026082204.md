## Tip 2: Prepare for Verification (KYC) Before You Start

Completing Know Your Customer (KYC) verification is mandatory for full account functionality. Preparing in advance speeds up the process significantly.

### Have Your Documents Ready
Ensure you have a valid, government-issued ID on hand:
1.  **Primary Choice**: Passport. It is the most universally accepted document on international platforms.
2.  **Alternative**: Mainland China Resident Identity Card. Have both the front and back ready for clear photos.
3.  **Ensure Clarity**: All details, including your name, ID number, and expiration date, must be legible in the photos you upload.

### Optimize Your Environment for Facial Verification
The facial recognition step is crucial and often where delays occur.
*   **Lighting**: Perform the verification in a well-lit room. Avoid backlighting (e.g., sitting with a window behind you).
*   **Stability**: Use a stand or prop up your phone to keep it steady. Follow the on-screen instructions to turn your head slowly.
*   **App**: Use the official Binance mobile app for this step, as it is generally more optimized for the process than the web version.

## Tip 4: Immediate Post-Registration Security Setup

Do not wait to secure your account. The minutes right after registration are the most critical.

This is your account's most important security layer.
*   **App-Based 2FA (Recommended)**: Download an authenticator app like **Google Authenticator** or **Authy**. Link it to your Binance account in the security settings. It generates time-based codes that are more secure than SMS.
*   **SMS Authentication**: Can be used as a secondary method, but is vulnerable to SIM-swap attacks.

### Set an Anti-Phishing Code
This unique code will be included in all genuine emails from Binance.
*   **Function**: It allows you to instantly distinguish legitimate Binance communications from phishing emails.
*   **Setup**: Find this option in your account's "Security" settings. Create a personal code that you will remember.

### Review Device and Login Management
After logging in, check the "Device Management" section. You can see all devices logged into your account and remove any unfamiliar ones. Consider whitelisting withdrawal addresses for an extra layer of protection on your funds.
