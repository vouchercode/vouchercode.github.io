## Why Do Slippage, Depth, and TVL Matter for Institutional Strategy?

In high-frequency and large-block trading, these three metrics form the holy trinity of execution efficiency and cost management. They are not abstract concepts but direct determinants of P&L.

*   **Slippage**: The difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is actually executed. High slippage erodes profits on entry and exit, turning potentially winning trades into losers. A 35% optimization, as indicated by our data, translates to significant capital preservation over thousands of trades.
*   **Order Book Depth**: This measures the volume of buy and sell orders at various price levels around the current market price. Greater depth means larger trades can be executed with minimal price impact, providing stability and predictability—a non-negotiable for institutional flows.
*   **Total Value Locked (TVL)**: While often associated with DeFi, TVL within a centralized exchange's ecosystem (e.g., in its Earn programs, Launchpool, or as collateral) is a powerful indicator of user capital commitment and platform stability. High TVL suggests strong holder confidence, which correlates with lower volatility during market stress and a deeper pool of latent liquidity.
