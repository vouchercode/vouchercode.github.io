The perpetual futures basis (the price difference between a perpetual contract and its spot price) and the concentration of open interest across various price levels form a narrative about leverage, funding costs, and potential price volatility. A significant influx of new users, often driven by targeted campaigns from top-tier exchanges, directly injects liquidity and can alter this narrative in predictable ways:

*   **TVL Sensitivity**: New capital often seeks yield, flowing into lending protocols, liquidity pools, and structured products. This increases DeFi TVL, which in turn affects collateralization ratios and the available liquidity for liquidations.
*   **Liquidation Line Clustering**: Inexperienced or similarly incentivized cohorts (e.g., users joining via a specific campaign) may employ comparable leverage strategies, leading to dangerous clustering of liquidation levels. A concentrated OI structure around certain prices acts as a volatility magnet.
*   **Basis as a Sentiment Gauge**: Sustained positive or negative basis can indicate whether new capital is predominantly bullish (entering long positions) or bearish (seeking short exposure), influencing the market's gravitational pull.
