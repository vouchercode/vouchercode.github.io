## Why Do Withdrawals Get Stuck? The Core Issues Revealed

The investigation identified several key, often interconnected, factors that cause withdrawals to be delayed, frozen, or require additional verification:

*   **Enhanced Global Compliance Scrutiny:** Post-2025 regulatory frameworks have mandated much stricter Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF) checks. Withdrawals to new or unverified addresses now trigger multi-layered automated and manual reviews.
*   **Wallet Address & Network Mismatches:** A significant percentage of failed withdrawals stem from users selecting the wrong blockchain network (e.g., sending BEP-20 tokens to an ERC-20 address) or entering an incorrect destination address.
*   **Suspicious Activity Flags:** Sudden large-volume withdrawals, withdrawals to newly created or high-risk wallets, or patterns inconsistent with a user's typical behavior can automatically place an account under temporary review.
*   **Insufficient or Expired KYC:** While basic KYC allows for trading, higher withdrawal limits or certain fiat channels require advanced verification. An expired document (like a passport) can also halt the process.

> **Proactive Security Check:** Before initiating any withdrawal, always double-check the destination address and network. Bookmark the official Binance support page for the latest status updates on network congestion: [Binance Support Status](https://www.binance.com/en/support/announcement/)

## The Step-by-Step Withdrawal Process & Where It Fails (2026 Guide)

### Step 1: Initiating the Withdrawal Request
Navigate to 'Wallet' -> 'Fiat and Spot' -> 'Withdraw'. Select the cryptocurrency or fiat currency you wish to withdraw.
*   **Critical Note:** The system performs an initial risk assessment at this point. If your account has any pending tickets or recent security changes, delays may begin here.

### Step 2: Entering Destination Details
This is the most error-prone stage. You must enter the correct external wallet address and select the corresponding **blockchain network**.
1.  **For Crypto:** Paste the address from your external wallet. **Triple-check the first and last 5 characters.** Mismatched networks lead to permanent loss of funds.
2.  **For Fiat:** Provide your verified bank account details. Ensure the name matches your Binance KYC exactly.

### Step 3: Security Verification
You will need to pass all enabled security measures:
1.  **Email/SMS Verification:** A code is sent to your registered contact method.
2.  **2FA Authentication:** Enter the code from your authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator).
3.  **Withdrawal Whitelist:** If enabled, the address must be pre-approved and have passed its security hold period.

### Step 4: The "Pending Review" Black Box
After submission, the request enters a processing queue. The investigation found that requests flagged by automated systems for "manual review" can take from **2 hours to 72+ hours**, depending on case complexity and support backlog. Users receive a generic "under review" notification with no specific timeline.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: My withdrawal has been "Processing" for over 24 hours. Are my funds safe?**
A: Yes. Funds are debited from your spot wallet and held in a secure, pending status during review. They are not lost. The delay is due to security and compliance checks.

**Q: I entered the wrong network. Can Binance recover my crypto?**
A: Generally, no. Transactions on a blockchain are irreversible. Binance has no control over funds sent to an address on an incompatible network. This underscores the critical importance of verifying the network before confirming.

**Q: Why was I asked for a source of funds proof after years of using Binance?**
A: Regulatory requirements are dynamic and retroactive. A large withdrawal or a deposit from a newly blacklisted service may trigger a one-time request for documentation to maintain compliance with global standards.

**Q: How can I avoid these issues in the future?**
A: Practice proactive security: complete full KYC, use the withdrawal whitelist, withdraw to familiar addresses, and keep your account documents up-to-date. Plan large withdrawals outside of periods of high market volatility, when review systems are under heavier load.
