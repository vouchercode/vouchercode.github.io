## The System That Saved Me (And My Sanity)

The recovery process, while successful, took over 72 hours. During that time, I couldn't sleep. I was consumed by "what-ifs." That's when I decided that operational security (OpSec) couldn't just be about strong passwords and 2FA; it had to be a holistic, foolproof system for every transaction. Here’s the exact protocol I developed, which has since saved me from countless potential errors and, through efficiency, saved me money equivalent to a high-end laptop.

### Step One: The Pre-Deposit Checklist
Before I even copy an address, I run through this mental list:
1.  **Asset & Network Confirmation:** On the exchange, I identify the *exact* asset (e.g., USDT) and the *supported network* (e.g., ERC-20, TRC-20). I do not assume; I verify from the official deposit page.
2.  **Wallet Reconciliation:** In my external wallet (like MetaMask or Trust Wallet), I triple-check that I have selected the corresponding network and asset type.
3.  **The "Test Transaction" Rule:** For any new deposit address or large sum, I *always* send a minimum test amount first. The small fee is my best-paid insurance.

### Step Two: The Copy-Paste Ritual
I never type an address manually. I use the exchange's "Copy" button. Then, I paste it into a simple text editor (like Notepad) first. This breaks any potential malicious script and lets me visually compare the first and last 5 characters of the address with the one on the exchange screen.

### Step Three: Post-Transaction Verification
Once I send, I don't just close the tab.
1.  I immediately take the transaction ID (TXID) from my wallet.
2.  I go to the relevant blockchain explorer (Etherscan for ERC-20, etc.).
3.  I confirm the transaction status and that it is headed to the correct destination address. Only then do I consider the process initiated.

## Essential Step: Beyond Deposits – Withdrawal Safety

Deposits are only half the battle. Withdrawals are where you reclaim your assets, and the rules are even stricter.
1.  **White-list Addresses:** I use the exchange's whitelisting feature religiously. Any address I withdraw to must be added and verified (which takes 24-48 hours) beforehand. This prevents any last-minute changes to a hacker's address.
2.  **Network Double-Check (Again!):** Ensuring the withdrawal network matches my receiving wallet's network is just as critical here.
3.  **2FA for Every Action:** No withdrawal is processed without confirming it via my authenticator app. Email confirmations are a secondary, not primary, layer.
