## OKX Coin Transfer Detailed Steps (Screenshot Guide)

### Step 1: Log In and Navigate to 'Withdraw'
Log into your OKX account via the app or website. On the main dashboard, locate and tap the **"Assets"** tab, then select **"Withdraw"**.

* **Note**: Ensure you are on the official OKX website or have downloaded the legitimate app from official stores to prevent phishing.

### Step 2: Select the Cryptocurrency
In the withdrawal section, you will see a list of available coins. **Click on the cryptocurrency** you wish to transfer (e.g., Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), USDT).

* **Important**: Confirm you are selecting the correct coin ticker. Transferring BTC to an ETH address will result in loss.

### Step 3: Enter the Recipient's Withdrawal Address
This is the most critical step. **Paste the recipient's wallet address** into the "Address" field.
* **Best Practice**: Always use copy-paste. Manually typing a long string of characters is error-prone.
* **Double-Check**: Verify the first 4 and last 4 characters of the pasted address match the intended recipient's address.

### Step 4: Select the Transfer Network
You will see a dropdown menu for **"Network"**. This choice is vital.
* **Match Networks**: The network you select on OKX **MUST** match the network supported by the receiving wallet (e.g., ERC20 for Ethereum, TRC20 for Tron, BTC for Bitcoin).
* **Fee & Speed**: Different networks have different withdrawal fees and confirmation times. The interface usually displays the fee.

### Step 5: Enter the Transfer Amount
Input the amount you wish to send. You can often click "Max" to withdraw all available balance (minus the network fee).
* **Check Minimums**: Ensure your amount meets the minimum withdrawal requirement displayed on the page.
* **Fee Deduction**: Note that the received amount will be your sent amount minus the network fee.

### Step 6: Review All Details and Confirm
Before the final step, a summary screen will appear. **SCRUTINIZE EVERY DETAIL**:
1.  Recipient Address
2.  Network
3.  Amount
4.  Estimated Fee
5.  Total Deduction (Amount + Fee)

If everything is correct, proceed to the security verification.

### Step 7: Complete Security Verification
OKX will prompt for 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication). This is typically:
1.  A code from your authenticator app (like Google Authenticator).
2.  Or an email/SMS verification code.
Enter the code and submit. You will receive a confirmation that the withdrawal request has been submitted.

## Post-Transfer: What to Expect and Do

1.  **Processing Status**: The transfer will move from "Processing" to "Completed" on OKX. You can track this under "Withdrawal History".
2.  **On-Chain Confirmation**: The transaction is only final after it receives confirmations on the blockchain. This can take from a few minutes to over an hour, depending on network congestion.
3.  **Save the TxID**: Once submitted, a Transaction ID (TxID/Hash) is generated. **Save this**. It is your proof of transaction and allows you to track it on a blockchain explorer (like Etherscan for ETH).

## Common Questions & Troubleshooting (FAQ)

**Q: I selected the wrong network. What can I do?**
A: Unfortunately, if coins are sent via an incompatible network (e.g., USDT via ERC20 to a TRC20 address), they are likely lost permanently. This is why Step 4 is critical. Contact OKX support, but recovery is often impossible.

**Q: How long does a withdrawal usually take?**
A: OKX processing is usually quick (minutes). The delay is typically due to blockchain confirmations. Bitcoin transactions can be slower than Tron or Solana transfers.

**Q: My transaction is stuck as "Processing" for a long time.**
A: First, check the blockchain using the TxID. If it's confirmed on-chain but not in your OKX account, contact OKX customer support with the TxID for assistance.

**Q: Are the withdrawal fees fixed?**
A: No. Fees are determined by the blockchain network, not OKX. They fluctuate based on network congestion. OKX displays the fee before you confirm.
