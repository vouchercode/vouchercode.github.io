## Why is Cross-Chain Migration a Critical Pain Point for Institutions in 2026?

The imperative for cross-chain functionality is driven by a fragmented yet interconnected ecosystem. Institutions cannot afford to be siloed on a single network when liquidity, yield opportunities, and innovative financial instruments are distributed across multiple chains like Ethereum, Solana, Avalanche, and various Layer-2 solutions. The pain, however, lies in the execution:

*   **Fragmented Liquidity & Slippage:** Bridging large volumes often means navigating a maze of decentralized bridges and centralized custodians, each with its own liquidity pools. This fragmentation leads to high slippage, directly impacting the final settlement value of the migrated assets.
*   **Security Vulnerabilities:** The cross-chain bridge sector has been a prime target for exploits. Institutions face the daunting task of auditing and trusting the security models of multiple third-party bridge protocols, where a single vulnerability can lead to catastrophic loss.
*   **Compliance & Audit Trail Obfuscation:** Moving assets across chains can break the transparent audit trail that regulators and internal compliance teams require. Tracking the origin, destination, and counterparties in a multi-hop cross-chain transaction becomes exponentially more complex.
*   **Operational Overhead & Time Cost:** Manual processes, multiple wallet interactions, waiting for bridge confirmations, and managing gas fees on different networks consume valuable operational resources and time, leading to missed opportunities.
