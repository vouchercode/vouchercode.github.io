### 1. Maker/Taker Fee Model
This is the core transaction fee. Your role in providing or taking liquidity determines the rate.
*   **Maker Fee**: Charged when you place an order that does not immediately match with an existing order (e.g., a limit order placed away from the current market price). This fee is typically lower, as you add liquidity to the order book.
*   **Taker Fee**: Charged when you place an order that immediately matches an existing order (e.g., a market order). This fee is usually higher.

**The 2026 Calculation Efficiency**: Bitget's system automatically classifies your order in real-time. The most time-saving aspect is that these rates are transparently displayed before order confirmation and are calculated as:
`Fee = Order Value * Fee Rate`.

### 2. Funding Fee Mechanism
Unique to perpetual contracts, this fee is exchanged periodically (e.g., every 8 hours) between long and short position holders to keep the contract price aligned with the spot market index.
*   **Calculation**: `Funding Fee = Position Value * Funding Rate`. The Funding Rate is variable and determined by the premium between the contract price and the spot index.
*   **The "Effort-Saving" Insight**: Monitoring the funding rate schedule and rate predictions can help you time your entries and exits. Some advanced traders use automated tools or bots to manage positions around funding windows, saving manual effort.
