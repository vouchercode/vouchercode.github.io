## Why is Your Binance Deposit Not Showing Up?

Before diving into solutions, it's crucial to understand the common reasons behind delayed or missing deposits. This knowledge can save you time and anxiety.

*   **Blockchain Network Congestion:** During periods of high traffic (like major market movements), transactions can get stuck in the mempool, causing significant delays in confirmations. Your funds are in transit but moving slowly.
*   **Insufficient Network Confirmations:** Each blockchain requires a specific number of confirmations before an exchange like Binance can credit the deposit. If you check immediately after sending, it will likely still be "pending."
*   **Incorrect Deposit Address or Memo/Tag:** This is a critical error. Sending assets to a wrong address or forgetting/entering an incorrect memo (for networks like XRP, XLM, ATOM, etc.) means the funds are sent to a location Binance cannot access.
*   **Depositing Unsupported Tokens:** Sending a token via a network that Binance does not support for that specific asset (e.g., sending USDT via the Polygon network to a Binance Ethereum USDT address) will result in the funds not being credited.
*   **Platform Processing Delay:** Rarely, Binance's internal systems might experience a brief backlog in processing a large volume of incoming transactions.

## Step-by-Step Troubleshooting Guide (Get Your Funds Credited)

Follow this systematic approach to diagnose and resolve your deposit issue.

### Step 1: Verify the Transaction on the Blockchain
This is the most important step. Do not rely solely on your wallet's "Sent" status.
1.  Find the **Transaction ID (TxID)** or **Hash** from your sending wallet or exchange.
2.  Go to the relevant blockchain explorer (e.g., Etherscan for ETH/ERC20, BscScan for BEP20, Blockchain.com for BTC).
3.  Paste the TxID and check:
    *   **Status:** Is it confirmed/pending/failed?
    *   **Confirmations:** How many confirmations are there? Compare this to Binance's required threshold.
    *   **Recipient Address:** Does it **exactly** match your Binance deposit address (and memo/tag if applicable)?

### Step 2: Check the Binance Deposit History
Log into your Binance account.
1.  Navigate to **Wallet** -> **Overview** -> **Transaction History**.
2.  Select **Deposit** and find the asset you deposited.
3.  Check the status here: **Processing, Successful, or Failed**. The status here often provides a specific reason for any delay.

### Step 3: Cross-Check Deposit Information
*   **Address/Memo:** Manually compare the address and memo you sent to with the one generated by Binance. Even a single character difference matters.
*   **Network:** Did you select the correct network (e.g., ERC20, BEP20, TRC20) both in your sending platform and on Binance? They must match.

### Step 4: Wait for Sufficient Confirmations
If the blockchain shows the transaction is confirmed but Binance still shows "Processing," simply wait. Most delays resolve automatically once the required confirmation count is met. This can take from a few minutes to several hours during network congestion.

### Step 5: Contact Binance Support (If All Else Fails)
If 24 hours have passed with sufficient confirmations on the blockchain and your funds are still not credited, it's time to contact support.
1.  Go to Binance's **Support Center**.
2.  Use the live chat or submit a ticket.
3.  **Provide these details clearly:**
    *   Your Binance UID.
    *   The exact amount and type of cryptocurrency deposited.
    *   The sending address (from your wallet).
    *   The receiving address (your Binance address).
    *   The Transaction ID (TxID/Hash).
    *   Screenshots of the transaction from the blockchain explorer.
