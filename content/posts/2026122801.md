## The "Japan Fund" Review Framework: A Step-by-Step Guide

This is the exact template my brother adopted. He dedicates 30 minutes at the end of each trading day to this ritual, without fail.

### Step 1: The Trade Log (The Raw Data)
Every trade gets a dedicated entry in a spreadsheet or journal app. The fields are non-negotiable:
1.  **Date/Time:** Of entry and exit.
2.  **Asset:** Which cryptocurrency.
3.  **Direction:** Long or Short.
4.  **Entry Price & Reasoning:** *Why* did you enter? (e.g., "Break of key resistance at $X on 4H chart," "RSI divergence on daily," "Strong fundamental news on project Y").
5.  **Exit Price & Reasoning:** *Why* did you exit? (e.g., "Hit profit target at $Z," "Stop-loss triggered due to unexpected macro news," "Emotional exit on a dip").
6.  **P/L (Profit/Loss):** In both percentage and USD terms.
7.  **Emotional State:** Honest rating (1-5) of greed/fear/anxiety/confidence at entry and exit.

### Step 2: The Weekly Analysis (Connecting the Dots)
Every Sunday, he reviews the week's log. This is where insights emerge:
*   **Win Rate & Profit Factor:** What percentage of trades were profitable? Is the average winner larger than the average loser?
*   **Context is Key:** He asks: "What was the overall market trend (BTC dominance, fear & greed index) when I took these trades?" A winning trade in a bear market is very different from one in a bull market.
*   **Correlating Emotion with Outcome:** He created a simple chart. He found an undeniable inverse correlation: trades executed with an "emotional state" score above 4 (high excitement/greed) had a 70% failure rate.

### Step 3: The Monthly Strategic Pivot (The Payout)
This is the action phase. Based on the monthly data, he makes one deliberate adjustment to his strategy for the next month.
*   **Month 1 Finding:** He was overtrading. Too many small, impulsive positions.
*   **Monthly Pivot:** He imposed a hard rule: Maximum of 3 open spot positions at any time. Quality over quantity.
*   **Month 2 Finding:** His "high-conviction" trades based on deep fundamental research had an 80% success rate, while "technical bounce" plays were near 50/50.
*   **Monthly Pivot:** He allocated 70% of his capital to the high-conviction model and 30% to a separate, smaller "technical play" account. This compartmentalization was a game-changer.
