## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: Is it safe to give my card details to the third-party buy service?**
A: The services integrated by Bitget Wallet (e.g., MoonPay) are licensed, regulated financial technology providers specializing in crypto on-ramps. They use bank-level encryption and compliance. Your full card details are not stored by Bitget Wallet itself.

**Q: The card fee is 3%. Isn't that high compared to slippage savings?**
A: They are different cost layers. The card fee is the cost of *entering* the market with fiat. Once your capital is in crypto, every subsequent trade incurs network gas fees and potential slippage. Minimizing slippage on frequent trades, especially large ones, can save far more in the long run than a one-time entry fee. For larger amounts, bank transfer options (often lower fee) within the wallet are also available.

**Q: Can I unlink my card after purchasing?**
A: Yes. The "link" is not a permanent, data-sharing bridge. It simply means your verified card is remembered by the third-party provider for future faster checkouts. You can manage or remove this within the provider's interface or by using a private/incognito browser window for future purchases.

**Q: What if my swap fails due to low slippage tolerance?**
A: If you set slippage too low (e.g., 0.1%) for a volatile token, the transaction may fail as the price moves before confirmation. You will only lose the network gas fee for the failed transaction. Simply adjust the slippage tolerance slightly higher and retry.
