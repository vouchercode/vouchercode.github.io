## Technique 1: Strategic Network Selection for Crypto Deposits (The #1 Fee Saver)

When depositing cryptocurrencies like USDT, BTC, or ETH, the choice of blockchain network is paramount. Selecting the wrong network can result in lost funds or exorbitant fees.

### Step-by-Step Guide:
1.  **Initiate Deposit:** Log into your Bitget account, navigate to "Assets" > "Deposit".
2.  **Select Your Currency:** Choose the cryptocurrency you wish to deposit (e.g., USDT).
3.  **CRITICAL - Choose the Network:** Bitget will display multiple network options (e.g., Tron TRC20, Ethereum ERC20, BNB Smart Chain BEP20, Polygon, Arbitrum).
4.  **The Golden Rule:** **YOU MUST select the SAME network on Bitget that your sending wallet or exchange is using.** For example, if your USDT is on the Tron network in your external wallet, you must select "USDT-TRC20" on Bitget.
5.  **Copy the Address:** Use the provided deposit address and memo/tag (if required) exactly as shown.

> **Pro Tip:** For stablecoin transfers, **Tron (TRC20) or Polygon** networks typically offer the lowest transaction fees and fast confirmation times. Always compare the "Estimated Fee" shown by your sending platform before confirming.

## Technique 4: Timing & Batch Deposits (The Smart Capital Mover)

Fees, especially on networks like Ethereum, fluctuate with network congestion. Additionally, making numerous small deposits multiplies your fixed network costs.

### The Efficient Approach:
*   **Monitor Gas Fees:** Use sites like Etherscan's Gas Tracker to check Ethereum network fees. Schedule your ERC-20 token deposits during periods of "Low" or "Medium" activity, typically on weekends or late-night UTC hours.
*   **Batch Your Transfers:** Instead of depositing $100 daily, consolidate your funds and make a single, larger deposit weekly or monthly. This turns ten network fees into one, significantly reducing your overall cost basis.
*   **Plan for Trading:** Align your deposit schedule with your trading strategy to ensure funds are available when you need them, without incurring rush transfer premiums.

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them (Must-Read Checklist)

*   **Pitfall 1: Ignoring the Memo/Tag.** When depositing currencies like XRP, XLM, or EOS, forgetting the mandatory "Memo" or "Tag" will cause your funds to be lost. **Always double-check.**
*   **Pitfall 2: Sending from a Smart Contract.** Some wallets or DeFi platforms send funds via smart contracts. Bitget may not credit these automatically. Always send **from a standard personal wallet address**.
*   **Pitfall 3: Minimum Deposit Ignorance.** Sending an amount below the network's minimum deposit threshold listed on Bitget will not be credited. Check the "Deposit Minimum" before sending.
*   **Pitfall 4: Impatience with Confirmations.** After sending crypto, wait for the required number of blockchain confirmations. Do not open support tickets immediately; use the blockchain explorer to track your transaction.
