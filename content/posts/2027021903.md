3.  **Verify Your Target Wallet Address:** Double-check, then triple-check the withdrawal address. Use the address book feature for frequently used wallets. One wrong character means permanent loss.

### Step 2: Navigating to the Withdrawal Interface
1.  Log into your OKX account on the web platform or mobile app.
2.  Navigate to **'Assets'** > **'Withdraw'**.
3.  Select the cryptocurrency you wish to withdraw (e.g., BTC, ETH, USDT).

### Step 3: Configuring the Withdrawal for Maximum Speed
This is where mastery comes in. The withdrawal form has key levers:
1.  **Network Selection (CRITICAL):** This is the most important choice. If withdrawing USDT, you'll see options like **ERC-20, TRC-20, OKTC, etc.**
    *   For **lowest fees & high speed,** **TRC-20** or **OKTC** (OKX's own chain) are typically winners. Confirm the receiving wallet supports the same network.
    *   **ERC-20** is universal but often has higher gas fees and slower Ethereum base layer times.
2.  **Withdrawal Amount:** Enter the amount. Be mindful of the minimum withdrawal limit displayed.
3.  **Fee Tier:** OKX often offers different speed/fee tiers (e.g., "Standard," "Fast," "Custom"). In 2026, the "Fast" option is usually worth the small extra fee for time-sensitive moves. "Custom" lets advanced users set their own gas.

### Step 4: Final Verification and Execution
1.  Review all details: Amount, Address, Network, and Fee.
2.  Complete the security verification (2FA code, email/SMS confirmation if enabled).
3.  Click **'Submit'**. You will receive a submission confirmation. The transaction ID (TxID) will be generated.

### Step 5: Post-Submission Tracking
1.  Go to **'Assets'** > **'Withdrawal History'**.
2.  Find your transaction and click on the TxID. This will open the transaction on the relevant blockchain explorer (like OKLink, Etherscan, Tronscan).
3.  Here, you can track the real-time status: "Pending," "Processing," "Confirmed." With OKTC or TRC-20, confirmations often happen in under a minute.

**Q: I submitted a withdrawal but it's still "Processing." What's happening?**
A: "Processing" means OKX has approved it and the transaction is broadcast to the blockchain. The delay now is for on-chain confirmations. Check the TxID on the explorer. For networks like OKTC, this is very fast. For Ethereum during high traffic, it may take longer.

**Q: I chose the wrong network! What do I do?**
A: This is a critical error. If you sent funds to a correct address but on an unsupported network (e.g., sending USDT via ERC-20 to a TRC-20-only address), the funds are likely lost. **Contact OKX Support immediately** with the TxID. **Q: Are there any daily withdrawal limits?**

**Q: Why is the withdrawal fee different for the same asset?**
A: The fee is primarily determined by the **network** you select, not OKX. Blockchain network gas/energy fees fluctuate. OKTC and TRC-20 consistently offer the most stable and low-cost fees.
