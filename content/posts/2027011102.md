## Why is Using Leverage Like "Security Deposit and Three Months' Rent in Advance"?

The traditional rental model in many places requires a significant upfront cost: one month's rent as a security deposit plus the first and last months' rent. This locks up capital but grants you the right to live in a property worth many times that amount. Leverage trading operates on a strikingly similar principle.

*   **Your Margin is Your Security Deposit:** When you open a 5x leveraged position on Bitget, you only need to commit 20% of the total position's value. This 20% is your "security deposit." It's the capital you risk to control a position five times larger.
*   **Funding Fees are Your "Monthly Rent":** In perpetual swap contracts, a mechanism called the funding rate exists to tether the contract price to the spot price. This fee is exchanged between long and short positions periodically (e.g., every 8 hours). If you are on the side paying the fee, it acts like a recurring "rent" for maintaining your leveraged position. The cost is typically a small percentage.
*   **The "Three Months in Advance" Mindset:** Just as paying rent upfront requires planning, entering a leveraged trade demands preparation. You must have sufficient capital (margin) to cover potential volatility (maintenance margin) to avoid being "evicted" (liquidated). The initial capital outlay, while smaller than the full position, is still a committed sum.

The core of the analogy lies in **capital efficiency**. You use a controlled amount of your own capital (the deposit/margin) to access and benefit from a much larger asset's price movement. The goal is for the asset's appreciation (or depreciation, if shorting) to far exceed the cost of the "rent" (funding fees).

## Risk Management: The "Lease Agreement" for Your Trades

Just as a lease has clauses about damages and early termination, leverage trading has non-negotiable rules.
*   **Liquidation Price:** This is your "eviction notice." If the market moves against you and your margin falls below the maintenance threshold, your position will be automatically closed to prevent further loss. Always know this price.
*   **Stop-Loss & Take-Profit:** These are your customized "lease break terms." Setting these orders automatically closes your position at predefined levels, locking in profits or capping losses. **Never trade without them.**
*   **Isolated vs. Cross Margin:** Choose "Isolated Margin" (risk is limited to the margin allocated to that specific position) for beginners. It's like insuring only one rental property.
