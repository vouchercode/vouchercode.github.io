## Binance Withdrawal Detailed Steps (Screenshot Guide)

### Step 1: Log In & Navigate to Withdrawal
Log into your Binance account and hover over the **'Wallet'** menu in the top navigation bar. From the dropdown, select **'Fiat and Spot'** or **'Overview'**, then click on the **'Withdraw'** button.
*   **Note**: Ensure you are on the official Binance website (`binance.com`) and that your connection is secure (look for `https://`).

### Step 2: Select the Cryptocurrency
In the withdrawal interface, you will see a search bar or a list of assets. **Type or select the cryptocurrency** you wish to withdraw (e.g., BTC, ETH, USDT).
*   **Critical**: Pay close attention to the available networks for the chosen asset in the next step.

### Step 3: Enter the Withdrawal Address & Network
1.  In the **'Address'** field, paste the *exact* receiving address from your external wallet or another exchange.
2.  In the **'Network'** field, select the blockchain network that matches the one supported by your receiving platform. **Choosing the wrong network will result in lost funds.**
    *   *Example*: If withdrawing USDT to a MetaMask wallet on the Ethereum network, you must select the **ERC20** network. For a Tron wallet, select **TRC20**.

### Step 4: Enter the Withdrawal Amount
Input the amount you wish to withdraw. You can click **'Max'** to withdraw your entire available balance (minus the network fee). The interface will show you the estimated **network fee** and the **total amount you will receive**.

### Step 5: Security Verification
Binance will require you to complete one or more security verification steps:
1.  **Email/SMS Verification**: A code will be sent to your registered email or phone number.
2.  **2FA Authentication**: Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app (e.g., Google Authenticator).
3.  **Withdrawal Whitelist**: If enabled, you can only withdraw to pre-verified addresses for added security.

### Step 6: Review and Confirm
**Carefully review all details one final time**: The cryptocurrency, amount, destination address, and network. Once confirmed, click the final **'Confirm'** or **'Withdraw'** button.

### Step 7: Transaction Processing & Completion
After confirmation, the transaction will be submitted to the blockchain. You can track its status under **'Wallet' > 'Transaction History' > 'Withdraw'**. Processing times vary by network congestion.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How long does a withdrawal take?**
A: Processing time on Binance's side is usually fast. The main delay comes from blockchain network confirmations. Bitcoin (BTC) can take 10-60 minutes, while Ethereum (ERC20) or other networks may take from 2 minutes to 30 minutes depending on gas fees and congestion.

**Q: What happens if I choose the wrong network?**
A: If you send crypto via an incompatible network (e.g., sending USDT via the BEP20 network to an ERC20-only address), the funds will be lost permanently. **Always double-check the network.**

**Q: Why is my withdrawal pending or under review?**
A: Binance performs routine security reviews. This is normal for large withdrawals, first-time withdrawals to a new address, or during periods of high security alert. It usually resolves within 24 hours. Ensure your account verification (KYC) is complete.

**Q: Can I cancel a withdrawal?**
A: You can only cancel a withdrawal if its status is still **'Processing'** on Binance. Once the transaction is broadcast to the blockchain ('Completed'), it cannot be cancelled.
