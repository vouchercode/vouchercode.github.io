This is the core of the scam. Once you dive in, you encounter the real mechanics:
1.  **Impermanent Loss (The Silent Killer)**: Most "high yields" come from providing liquidity in DeFi pools. When the prices of the paired assets fluctuate—which they always do—you suffer impermanent loss. Your overall portfolio value in dollar terms can decrease even as you earn rewards, often negating the advertised APY.
2.  **Network Gas Fees**: Every interaction—staking, unstaking, claiming rewards, swapping tokens—requires a gas fee on the respective blockchain (Ethereum, BSC, etc.). These fees, especially during network congestion, can devour a significant portion of your small earnings.
3.  **Withdrawal Fees and Lock-Up Periods**: Some pools have unbonding periods where your assets are locked and earning nothing. Others charge a flat percentage on withdrawal. The "high yield" is gross, not net.
4.  **Slippage and Swap Fees**: To enter these pools, you often need to swap tokens. Bitget Wallet's built-in swap function, while convenient, may not offer the best rate. The slippage and the spread between buy/sell prices constitute a hidden cost.
