## Binance Deposit Detailed Steps (Screenshot Tutorial)

### Step 1: Log In & Navigate to the Deposit Section
First, log into your Binance account. **Crucial Reminder:** Always double-check the website URL is `https://www.binance.com` to avoid phishing sites. Once logged in, locate and click on **[Wallet]** -> **[Fiat and Spot]** -> **[Deposit]**.

*   **Pro Tip:** Bookmark the official Binance login page. Never click on deposit links from emails or messages unless you are 100% certain of their authenticity.

### Step 2: Select Your Deposit Currency
This is a critical step where beginners often make the first costly error.
1.  In the deposit interface, you will see a search bar to choose a currency.
2.  You can deposit **Cryptocurrency** (like BTC, ETH, USDT) or **Fiat Currency** (like USD, EUR, via bank transfer/card).
3.  **For Fiat (Cash):** Select your local currency (e.g., USD). The system will show you available payment partners and methods. **Compare the fees and processing times carefully.**
4.  **For Crypto:** Type the exact cryptocurrency ticker you wish to deposit (e.g., USDT). **HERE IS THE MAJOR PITFALL:** You MUST select the correct **network/blockchain**. For USDT, you might see options like BEP20 (Binance Smart Chain), ERC20 (Ethereum), TRC20 (Tron), etc.
    *   **Rule of Thumb:** If depositing from another exchange or a private wallet to Binance, using the **BEP20 (BSC) network is often the cheapest and fastest.** However, **ALWAYS CONFIRM** Binance supports deposits for that asset on that network. Sending to the wrong network will result in permanent loss of funds.

### Step 3: Generate Your Deposit Address/Instructions
*   **For Crypto:** After selecting the asset and correct network, Binance will generate a unique deposit address (a long string of characters) and a QR code. **You must use this exact address.**
*   **For Fiat:** You will be guided to a third-party payment processor (like Banxa or Simplex) or given bank details. Follow the on-screen instructions precisely.

### Step 4: Initiate the Transfer from Your Source
*   **From Another Wallet/Exchange:** Go to the withdrawal section of your source platform. Paste the Binance deposit address you copied in Step 3. **TRIPLE-CHECK** the address and network. Confirm the transaction. Be mindful of the withdrawal fee on the sending side.
*   **Via Bank/Card:** Enter the required amount and your payment details on the secured partner page. Ensure you complete any 3D Secure or bank authentication steps.

### Step 5: Wait for Confirmation
*   **Crypto:** Transaction speed depends on network congestion. BEP20/USDT can be very fast (a few minutes). Ethereum transactions may take longer and cost more in gas fees. You can track the progress using the provided Transaction ID (TxID) on a blockchain explorer.
*   **Fiat:** Processing can take from a few minutes (card) to several business days (bank transfer). The funds will appear in your Binance Fiat & Spot wallet once processed.

## The #1 Beginner Trap: Network Selection Errors

This deserves its own section. The single most common and devastating mistake is sending crypto on the wrong blockchain network. For example, sending **USDT on the ERC20 (Ethereum) network to a Binance address meant for BEP20 (BSC) deposits.** These networks are not compatible. If you make this error:
1.  Your transaction will be confirmed on its native blockchain (e.g., Ethereum).
2.  But Binance will not see it on the BSC network it was expecting.
3.  **The funds are effectively lost and unrecoverable in 99.9% of cases.**

**How to Avoid It:** Always match the network exactly. If Binance gives you a BEP20 address, ensure your sending platform is set to withdraw on the BEP20 (BSC) network. When in doubt, start with a **small test transaction**.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: I entered the wrong network for my deposit. What can I do?**
A: Unfortunately, if the crypto has been sent to an address on an unsupported network, recovery is extremely rare and not guaranteed. You must contact Binance Support immediately with the TxID. There may be a costly manual recovery process for some major assets, but never assume it's possible.

**Q: How long do deposits usually take?**
A: Crypto deposits depend on network confirmations (BSC: fast; Bitcoin: slower). Fiat deposits vary by method: credit/debit card (minutes), bank transfer (1-5 business days).

**Q: Are there deposit fees on Binance?**
A: Binance itself does not charge a fee for depositing crypto or fiat. However, network fees (for crypto) or processing fees charged by the third-party fiat service provider will apply. This is where choosing the right network (BEP20) saves money.

**Q: What is the minimum deposit amount?**
A: This varies by currency and method. The interface will show you the minimum before you proceed.
