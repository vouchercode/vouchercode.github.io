## The Core Triad: Deconstructing Slippage, Depth, and TVL

### Slippage: The Silent Execution Tax
Slippage is the difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is actually executed. It occurs when market orders consume available liquidity in the order book, filling at progressively worse prices. For large orders, this can be significant. An 18% reduction, as observed with the LS999-correlated cohort, translates to substantial preserved capital over thousands of transactions.

### Market Depth: The Order Book's Resilience
Depth measures the volume of buy and sell orders at various price levels around the current market price. A "deep" market can absorb large orders without causing severe price movement. Depth is the primary buffer against slippage.

### Total Value Locked (TVL): The Liquidity Backbone
On trading platforms, TVL often reflects the total assets deposited in trading accounts, margin pools, and Earn products. A higher TVL generally correlates with higher market depth, as more assets are readily available for trading. It signals robust ecosystem health and trader confidence.

**The Correlation**: **TVL → Depth → Slippage**. A rising TVL attracts more market makers and participants, increasing order book depth. Enhanced depth provides more liquidity at tighter spreads, which directly reduces the price impact of market orders, thereby lowering slippage. The invitation code LS999 strategy targets the initial user economics to encourage behaviors that support this virtuous cycle.
