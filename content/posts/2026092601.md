## The 8-Step Guide to One-Minute Verification (2026 Edition)

### Step 1: Choose the Right Platform and Time
Initiate verification directly on the platform's official website or its dedicated mobile app. The app is often optimized for faster camera access and processing.
* **Crucial**: Avoid peak hours (e.g., Monday mornings or platform launch times). Verification servers are less congested during off-peak periods, leading to quicker processing.

### Step 2: Prepare Your Documents in Advance
Have a valid, government-issued ID ready **before** you click "Start Verification." The accepted documents are typically:
1.  **International Passport** (Highest global acceptance rate).
2.  **National ID Card**.
3.  **Driver's License**.
Ensure the document is not expired, is physically present, and has clear, non-reflective text.

### Step 3: Optimize Your Environment and Lighting
This is the most critical step for automated AI approval. Poor lighting causes 70% of delays.
* **Lighting**: Use bright, diffused, and even front lighting. Face a window during the day or use a ring light. Avoid backlighting (e.g., sitting with a window behind you) and harsh shadows.
* **Background**: A plain, light-colored wall is ideal. Remove other people, clutter, or bright artwork from the frame.
* **Stability**: Use a phone stand or prop your device against something stable to prevent blurry images.

### Step 4: Master the Document Capture
When photographing your ID:
* **Lay it flat** on a dark, non-reflective surface.
* **Ensure all four corners** are visible within the guide frame on your screen.
* **Avoid glare** from overhead lights. Tilt the document slightly if needed.
* **Follow on-screen instructions** precisely—some systems require a video scan instead of a photo.

### Step 5: Ace the Liveness Check (Face Scan)
The "selfie" or video verification is where AI confirms you are a real person.
* **Follow the prompts exactly**: Turn your head, blink, or open your mouth only when instructed.
* **Remove accessories**: Take off hats, glasses (unless necessary), and face-obscuring headphones.
* **Maintain a neutral expression** and look directly into the camera. Ensure your face is fully within the oval guide.

### Step 6: Use a Stable, High-Speed Internet Connection
A poor connection can cause timeouts, failed uploads, or processing errors, forcing you to restart.
* **Connect to Wi-Fi** instead of mobile data whenever possible. If using mobile data, ensure you have full signal strength (4G/5G).
* **Close other bandwidth-heavy applications** (video streams, large downloads) on your device and network.

### Step 7: Double-Check All Entered Information
Manually re-enter your personal details (name, date of birth, address) from your ID document. An AI system will cross-check this data with the OCR (Optical Character Recognition) results from your ID photo.
* **Enter details exactly as they appear** on your document, including middle names and hyphens.
* **Use your current legal residential address**. A mismatch here is a common cause for manual review.

### Step 8: Submit and Be Patient (But Not for Long!)
Once submitted, do not refresh the page or restart the app. Most modern systems in 2026 provide a real-time status bar.
* **Automated Approval**: With perfect execution of Steps 1-7, many platforms now offer near-instant, AI-powered approval within 10-60 seconds.
* **If Pending**: If status shows "Under Review," wait at least 5-10 minutes. It may still be processed automatically. Only contact support if it remains pending for over 24 hours.

## Essential Post-Verification Security Setup

Verification gets you in the door; these settings keep your account safe:
* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**: Immediately set up 2FA using an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Authy). This is non-negotiable for account security.
* **Review Connected Devices**: Check your account security settings to see all logged-in devices and revoke access for any you don't recognize.
* **Set Up Anti-Phishing Codes**: If the platform offers it (like Binance), create a unique anti-phishing code. This code will appear in all genuine emails from the platform, helping you spot fakes.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: The AI keeps rejecting my document photo. What should I do?**
A: This is almost always a lighting or glare issue. Re-do Step 3. Turn off the flash, change the angle of your light source, and ensure the entire document is flat and in focus. Try using the platform's mobile app if you were on the web version, or vice versa.

**Q: Is it safe to submit my passport and face scan to these platforms?**
A: Reputable, regulated platforms (like major exchanges or fintech apps) use bank-level encryption (AES-256) and store data on secure servers solely for compliance. They have a legal obligation to protect your data. Always verify you are on the official website (check the URL) before submitting anything.

**Q: My verification is stuck "In Review" for days. What now?**
A: First, check your email (and spam folder) for any requests for additional information. If there are none, contact customer support directly through the official platform channels. Have your referral ID (if used) and the email/phone number associated with the account ready to speed up the support process.

**Q: Can I use a PO Box for my address during verification?**
A: Almost never. KYC requires a verifiable residential address. Using a PO Box will likely cause a failure or trigger a request for proof of address (like a utility bill or bank statement).
