## The 2026 Swing Trader's Framework: A Step-by-Step Mindset

### Step 1: Ditch the "Diamond Hands" Cult Mentality
Your first trade is a mental one. Stop viewing your portfolio as a shrine and start seeing it as a dynamic engine. Emotional attachment to specific coins is the enemy of profit. A swing trader's loyalty is to the strategy and risk/reward ratio, not to a ticker symbol.

### Step 2: Master the Two Essential Charts
Swing trading is built on technical analysis (TA). You don't need to be a wizard, but you must understand:
1.  **Support and Resistance**: These are price levels where an asset consistently reverses direction. Buying near support and selling near resistance is the fundamental swing trade.
2.  **Moving Averages**: The 50-day and 200-day Simple Moving Averages (SMAs) are critical. Crossovers and price action relative to these lines signal potential trend changes and swing points.

### Step 3: The Entry, Exit, and Fail-Safe Plan
Every swing trade must have these three defined *before* execution:
*   **Entry Point**: The price at which you buy, ideally confirmed by a bounce off support or a breakout above resistance.
*   **Take-Profit (TP) Target**: Your predetermined sell price, usually at the next level of resistance. This locks in your gain.
*   **Stop-Loss (SL) Order**: A mandatory sell order placed below your entry point. This automatically limits your loss if the trade goes against you. **This is non-negotiable.**
