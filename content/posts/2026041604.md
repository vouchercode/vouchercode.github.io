## Why is Liquidity Depth the Core Metric for Institutional Trading?

For institutional traders, the quoted price is often just the starting point. The true cost of a trade is revealed at the moment of execution, heavily influenced by the order book's liquidity depth. Slippage—the difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is actually executed—can erode profits significantly on large orders.

*   **Market Impact**: A shallow order book means a large buy/sell order can "eat through" multiple price levels, pushing the price against the trader and increasing cost.
*   **Execution Certainty**: Deep liquidity allows for the execution of substantial orders with minimal price movement, providing predictability and reducing market impact costs.
*   **Strategy Viability**: Advanced strategies like arbitrage, basis trading, and algorithmic execution are only feasible on platforms with sufficient depth to handle the required volume without self-sabotage through slippage.
