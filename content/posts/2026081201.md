## The 7 Critical Pitfalls to Avoid During OKX KYC (2026 Edition)

### Pitfall 1: Using Blurry or Poor-Quality Document Photos
This is the single most common reason for rejection. The system's AI and manual reviewers need crystal-clear images.
*   **Do**: Use a plain, contrasting background. Ensure all four corners of your ID (passport, driver's license, or national ID) are visible with no glare or shadows. The text must be legible.
*   **Don't**: Submit photos with fingers covering details, taken from an angle, or where the document is out of focus. A screenshot of a document photo is almost always rejected.
*   **Screenshot Recommendation**: Take a screenshot *after* you capture the document photo within the OKX app but before submitting. This serves as proof of the clarity you provided.

### Pitfall 2: Mismatched Information Between Documents
Consistency is key. The name, date of birth, and address on your submitted ID must perfectly match the information you entered during registration.
*   **Do**: Double-check your registered name against your official ID. If you used a middle name on OKX, ensure it appears on your ID or omit it consistently.
*   **Don't**: Register with an English nickname (e.g., "Mike Zhang") while your passport shows your legal name ("Mingzhe Zhang"). Use your legal name exactly as it appears.
*   **Action**: Before starting KYC, review your OKX account profile settings to confirm the personal details.

### Pitfall 3: Failing the Liveness Check (Face Verification)
The liveness detection is sophisticated and designed to prevent spoofing with photos or videos.
*   **Do**: Perform the check in a well-lit, static environment. Follow the on-screen prompts (blink, turn head) naturally and steadily. Keep your face within the frame.
*   **Don't**: Wear hats, heavy glasses, or face coverings. Attempt verification in a moving vehicle or with poor lighting. Use someone else's likeness.
*   **Screenshot Recommendation**: You usually can't screenshot during the active face scan, but take one of the instruction screen outlining the steps as a reminder.

### Pitfall 4: Submitting an Expired or Unsupported Document
OKX only accepts valid, government-issued photo IDs. An expired document is an instant rejection.
*   **Do**: Check the expiration date on your passport or ID card well in advance. Ensure your document type is listed as acceptable for your region on OKX's help page.
*   **Don't**: Use student IDs, work badges, or damaged documents. Even a document expiring next month can sometimes cause issues—renew it first if possible.
*   **Checklist**: Valid Passport > National ID Card > Driver's License (region-dependent).

### Pitfall 5: Incorrect Proof of Address (if required)
For higher tiers or specific jurisdictions, a proof of address (POA) like a utility bill or bank statement is needed.
*   **Do**: Submit a document issued within the last 90 days. It must clearly show your full name and residential address (P.O. boxes are not accepted). The name/address must match your KYC info.
*   **Don't**: Submit an online bill printout without issuer details, an insurance document, or a handwritten letter.
*   **Pro-Tip**: A PDF bank or credit card statement is widely accepted. Ensure it's the official statement, not just a transaction notification.

### Pitfall 6: Using a VPN or Proxy During Verification
While a VPN might be used for access, it can trigger security flags during KYC, especially if the IP location doesn't match your document's country.
*   **Do**: Disconnect from any VPN or proxy service before starting the KYC process. Use a stable, direct internet connection.
*   **Don't**: Start verification while connected to a server in a different country. This can lead to an automatic failure or a prolonged "Under Review" status.
*   **Important**: Your IP location should be consistent with the country of your submitted ID.

### Pitfall 7: Submitting Multiple Times in Rapid Succession After Failure
Impatiently resubmitting the same faulty application will not help and may lengthen the review queue or trigger a security cooldown period.
*   **Do**: If rejected, carefully read the reason provided by OKX support via email or in-app notification. Correct the *specific* issue before resubmitting.
*   **Don't**: Immediately re-upload the same blurry photo or document. Take 24 hours to prepare a perfect new submission based on the feedback.
*   **Patience is Key**: The standard review time is 1-24 hours. Only contact support if it has been longer than 48 hours without an update.
