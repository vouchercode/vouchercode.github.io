## Why Should You Care About Withdrawal FX Loss?

The FX loss during withdrawal isn't a separate fee; it's the often-invisible cost baked into the exchange rate when you convert crypto to fiat (like TWD, USD, or EUR). Exchanges apply a spread between the global market rate and the rate they offer you. A wider spread means more loss for you.

*   **Compounded Cost:** For frequent traders or those making regular profit withdrawals, even a 0.1% higher loss rate compounds quickly over time, silently eroding returns.
*   **Path Dependency:** Some exchanges route withdrawals through specific banking partners or liquidity pools with less favorable rates, creating a "path cost" that is fixed and predictable.
*   **Transparency Test:** Exchanges that advertise low trading fees might be recouping revenue through wider spreads on fiat conversions. On-chain data doesn't lie.

## Essential Step: Auditing Your Own Withdrawal History

You don't need to be a data scientist to check this.

1.  **Gather Data:** Export your withdrawal history from your exchange for the past 3-6 months.
2.  **Find the Rate:** For each fiat withdrawal, note the crypto amount debited and the fiat amount received.
3.  **Check the Benchmark:** Use a historical price chart (e.g., CoinMarketCap) to find the market price of your crypto at the exact hour of your withdrawal.
4.  **Calculate:** [(Market Value - Received Fiat) / Market Value] * 100 = Your Effective FX Loss %.
5.  **Compare:** Stack your results against our findings: 0.7% (Binance baseline), 1.0% (MAX), or look for fixed-path penalties.
