## The Core Analogy: Leverage Trading = Renting an Apartment

Let's demystify leverage trading with a real-world parallel. Imagine you want to rent an apartment with a monthly rent of $1,000.

*   **Traditional Spot Trading (Buying Outright)**: This is like **buying the entire apartment**. You need the full capital—$1,000 for one month's exposure—to control that asset. Your potential profit (or loss) is based on the full value, but your upfront cost is 100%.
*   **Bitget Leverage Trading (Using Margin)**: This is like **renting the apartment with a "first and last month's rent" deposit**. The landlord (the exchange) allows you to control the $1,000 apartment by only putting down a fraction of the cost as collateral (margin). For instance, with 10x leverage, you only need $100 (the "last month's rent") to control the $1,000 position. Your profit/loss is still calculated on the full $1,000 value.

**The "Harsh Truth"**: Just as failing to pay rent can get you evicted and you lose your deposit, if your leveraged trade moves against you and your collateral (margin) is depleted, the exchange will liquidate your position to cover the loss. You lose your initial capital. This is the fundamental risk and responsibility that comes with the power of leverage.
