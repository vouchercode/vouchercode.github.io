## Method 1: The Foundation - Depositing Crypto to Your OKX Account

Depositing is how you bring external crypto into your OKX trading account.

### Step-by-Step Guide:
1.  **Log In & Navigate**: Log into your OKX account. Go to **Assets** > **Deposit**.
2.  **Select the Coin**: Search for and select the cryptocurrency you wish to deposit (e.g., BTC, ETH, USDT).
3.  **Choose the Network (CRITICAL STEP)**: You will see a list of supported networks (e.g., Bitcoin, ERC-20, TRC-20, Arbitrum One). **You must select the same network that the sender (or your external wallet) is using.** Sending via the wrong network will result in permanent loss of funds.
4.  **Copy the Deposit Address**: Click the copy button next to your unique deposit address. A QR code is also available for mobile wallets.
5.  **Initiate Transfer from Sender**: Go to your external wallet or exchange, paste the OKX deposit address, select the matching network, and confirm the transaction.
6.  **Wait for Confirmations**: The deposit will appear in your OKX account after the required number of blockchain confirmations.

> **⚠️ Warning**: Never deposit a coin on a network not explicitly listed for that coin on OKX. For example, do not send USDT via the Bitcoin network.

This is the most important security feature for preventing withdrawal mistakes or hacks.

*   **How it Works**: You pre-approve and save a list of trusted external wallet addresses. Withdrawals can only be sent to these whitelisted addresses.
*   **Setup**: Go to **Security Settings** > **Whitelist**. Add addresses with a unique label. There is typically a 24-48 hour holding period after adding a new address before it can be used.
*   **Benefit**: Even if your account password is compromised, thieves cannot withdraw funds to an address not on your whitelist.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: My deposit hasn't arrived after a long time. What should I do?**
A: First, check the transaction status (TXID) on the relevant blockchain explorer using the transaction ID from your sender. Confirm it has enough confirmations. If the network is congested, wait longer. If everything seems correct but the deposit isn't showing, contact OKX support with your TXID.

**Q: I withdrew to the wrong network. Can I recover my funds?**
A: Recovering funds sent to the wrong network is extremely difficult and often impossible. OKX does not have access to the private keys of addresses on other chains. This highlights the critical importance of double-checking the network.

**Q: Are there daily withdrawal limits?**
A: Yes, withdrawal limits depend on your account verification level. You can view and increase your limits by completing higher tiers of Identity Verification (KYC) in your account settings.

**Q: Why is the network fee for my withdrawal so high?**
A: Withdrawal fees are based on real-time blockchain network congestion. Fees are dynamic. You can sometimes choose a slower speed for a lower fee if the network supports it.
