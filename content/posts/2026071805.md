Let's walk through the modern ordeal of tracing a single, hypothetical trade on Binance in 2026.

You remember buying some SOL last week. You open the Binance app. Do you go to "Wallet" > "Overview"? Or "Trade" > "Spot"? The history isn't centralized. You start in "Wallet" and click on your SOL balance.

### Step 2: Entering the Labyrinth
Clicking "Transaction History" presents a filter menu: Deposit, Withdrawal, Buy, Sell, Fee, Transfer, etc. You select "Buy." A list appears with timestamps, amounts, and a status. One entry says "Completed" for 1.5 SOL. But at what price? What was the total USDT cost? This list doesn't say. It's just a ledger of asset inflows.

### Step 3: The Cross-Referencing Puzzle
You now need to find the corresponding *trade*. You exit the wallet, go to "Trade" > "Spot" > "Order History." You filter for SOL/USDT, past week. Here you find the limit order: "Filled, 1.5 SOL @ $185.43." Great! So the cost was ~$278.15 USDT. But your USDT wallet seems to have been debited $280.15. Where is the $2 difference?

### Step 4: The Fee Hunt
Back to "Transaction History." Add "Fee" to the filter. Lo and behold, a separate entry: "Fee - 0.1% - Deducted from BNB." But wait, you didn't have BNB balance pay enabled. You check your BNB transaction history. Nothing. You then check your USDT "Transaction History" and find a tiny, easy-to-miss entry: "Fee - $2.00 USDT." It was taken from your USDT balance because the BNB fee discount wasn't active. Mystery solved, after 10 minutes of digging.

### Step 5: The Final Audit
To get the complete picture for your own records, you now have to manually reconcile three separate log entries from three different parts of the interface: 1) The SOL asset inflow, 2) The trade execution details, 3) The fee deduction. This is your "statement."

**Q: I see a transaction with a "Memo" I don't recognize. Should I panic?**
A: Not immediately. Memos are often required for depositing to centralized exchange wallets (like Crypto.com or Kraken) or for certain staking protocols.
