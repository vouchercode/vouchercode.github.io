## The 2026 Bitget Hong Kong KYC Pitfall Avoidance Guide (Step-by-Step)

### Pitfall 1: Using an Unsupported ID Document
The most immediate rejection reason is submitting an invalid document. Bitget Hong Kong typically accepts:
*   **Hong Kong Permanent Identity Card (HKID)** – The most straightforward option for residents.
*   **Passport** – Must be valid, machine-readable, and from a recognized jurisdiction.
*   What **WON'T work**: Mainland China Resident Identity Card, expired documents, or driver's licenses from certain regions.
*   **Action**: Before uploading, check your document's expiry date and ensure all four corners are visible in your photo.

### Pitfall 2: Poor-Quality Document Photos or Screenshots
Blurry, glary, or cropped images are automatically flagged by the system.
*   **Do**: Take photos in a well-lit, neutral background. Lay the document flat. Ensure every piece of text, hologram, and photo is crystal clear.
*   **Don't**: Upload scanned PDFs, screenshots of screenshots, or photos with shadows/fingers covering details. *(Screenshot recommended here: Show a side-by-side comparison of a rejected blurry photo vs. an accepted clear one).*
*   **Action**: Use your smartphone's main camera, not a webcam. Enable the gridlines to keep the document straight.

### Pitfall 3: Incorrect Personal Information Matching
The details on your submitted ID must match **exactly** with the information you entered during Bitget account registration.
*   **Check**: Full name (in English/Pinyin as per passport, or in Chinese as per HKID), date of birth, and document number. Even a minor typo (e.g., "Lau" vs. "Liu") can cause a mismatch.
*   **Action**: Double-check your registered Bitget account profile before starting KYC. If you registered with a nickname, you must update it to your legal name first.

### Pitfall 4: Failing the Liveness Detection (Face Verification)
This step proves you are a real person holding your own document. Common failures include:
*   Poor lighting (too dark or backlit).
*   Wearing hats, glasses, or masks that obscure your face.
*   Moving too quickly or not following the on-screen prompts (e.g., blink, turn head).
*   **Action**: Perform verification in a consistently bright environment. Remove obstructions. Follow the audio/visual prompts patiently. Use a front-facing camera on a stable device.

### Pitfall 5: Submitting an Invalid Proof of Address (if required)
For enhanced verification tiers, a proof of address dated within the last 3 months is often required.
*   **Accepted**: Bank statements, utility bills (electricity, water), or government-issued tax letters. They must clearly show your name and residential address.
*   **Rejected**: Mobile phone bills, online shopping invoices, or any document older than 90 days.
*   **Action**: Prepare a digital copy (PDF or high-quality photo) of a valid utility bill in advance.

### Pitfall 6: Using a VPN or Proxy During Verification
Bitget's system detects your IP address. If you registered your account from one region (e.g., Hong Kong) but attempt KYC from a different IP location (e.g., via a US VPN), it raises a red flag for potential fraud.
*   **Action**: Disable all VPNs, proxies, or privacy tools before starting the KYC process. Ensure your internet connection reflects your actual physical location in Hong Kong.

### Pitfall 7: Submitting Multiple Times in Rapid Succession
If your application is rejected, the system provides a reason. Immediately re-submitting the same incorrect documents will not help and may trigger a security cooldown period, locking your account from further KYC attempts for 24-48 hours.
*   **Action**: Carefully read the rejection reason email. Correct the specific issue (e.g., "Document unclear," "Information mismatch") before submitting a new application.

### Pitfall 8: Ignoring the Post-Submission "Pending Review" Status
After submission, the status will show as "Under Review." The estimated time is usually within 1-2 business hours, but can extend to 24 hours during peak times.
*   **Don't**: Open multiple support tickets asking for an ETA immediately after submitting. This clogs the queue.
*   **Action**: Be patient. You will receive an email notification upon approval or if further action is required. Only contact support if the status hasn't changed after 48 hours.
