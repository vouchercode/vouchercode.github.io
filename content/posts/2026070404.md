### Step 2: Select the Futures Contract
Choose the cryptocurrency futures contract you wish to trade (e.g., BTC-USDT-SWAP). The grid bot works with perpetual swap contracts.
* **Tip for Beginners**: Start with a major pair like BTC or ETH, as they typically have higher liquidity and more predictable volatility ranges for grid strategies.

### Step 3: Set Your Trading Parameters (The Core Setup)
This is the most crucial step where you define the bot's strategy.
1.  **Grid Type**: Select "**Arithmetic**" for equal price intervals between orders, which is simpler and recommended for starters.
2.  **Price Range**: Set the **Lower Price** and **Upper Price**. The bot will only operate within this corridor. Use the market chart to identify a reasonable consolidation or sideways range.
3.  **Number of Grids**: This determines how many buy/sell orders the bot places within your range. More grids mean more potential trades from smaller price movements but require more initial capital. Start with 20-50 grids.
4.  **Investment Amount**: Input the amount of USDT you wish to allocate to the strategy. The system will calculate the required margin.

### Step 4: Advanced Settings (Optional but Recommended)
Click "**Advanced Settings**" to fine-tune your bot.
* **Stop-Loss / Take-Profit**: It's wise to set a stop-loss price to limit potential downside if the market breaks strongly below your lower price.
* **Trigger Price**: You can set the bot to activate only when the market price reaches a certain level.

### Step 5: Review the AI Parameters & Create the Bot

## Crucial Knowledge: Understanding Futures Grid Mechanics

A Futures Grid bot on OKX simultaneously holds a futures contract position and places opposing limit orders around it. Unlike a spot grid, it profits in two ways:
1.  **Grid Profits**: From the successful buy-low, sell-high cycles of its limit orders within the range.
2.  **Floating P&L**: From the directional movement of the underlying futures position itself.
This combination allows it to potentially profit in sideways, slightly trending, and even volatile markets, but it also changes the risk profile. The initial investment covers the futures margin, not the full notional value of the assets.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: What happens if the price breaks above my Upper Price or below my Lower Price?**
A: If the price goes above the upper limit, the bot will stop placing new sell orders and will hold a long futures position. If it falls below the lower limit, it stops placing buy orders and holds a short position. Your pre-set stop-loss (if configured) is critical here to limit losses.

**Q: Can I run multiple Futures Grid bots at once?**
A: Yes, you can run multiple bots on different contracts or with different parameters. However, ensure your total margin used does not exceed your risk tolerance.

**Q: Are the grid profits guaranteed?**
A: No. Grid profits are earned only when the price oscillates between your buy and sell orders within the set range. If the market moves in a single direction without fluctuation, you may not earn grid profits and may face a loss on the futures position.

**Q: How are fees handled?**
A: OKX charges a taker fee for the initial futures order when the bot starts and maker fees for most of the grid limit orders. Your net profit is calculated after all fees.
