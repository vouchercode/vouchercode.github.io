## Detailed Steps to Add Tokens to Your Bitget Wallet (2026 Guide)

The process is designed to be intuitive. Whether it's a newly launched meme coin or a established DeFi token, here’s how to make it appear in your wallet.

### Step 1: Open Your Bitget Wallet App
Launch the Bitget Wallet application on your mobile device. Ensure you are on the main "Assets" or "Wallet" screen, which displays your current token balances.
*   **Pro Tip**: For security, always download the official app from the Bitget website or verified app stores. Bookmark the official site to avoid phishing attempts.

### Step 2: Locate the "Add Token" or "Manage Tokens" Function
This button is typically represented by a **"+"** icon, a settings gear next to the token list, or a direct "Add Token" label. It is usually found on the main assets page.
1.  Look for it at the top-right corner of your asset list.
2.  Or, scroll through your token list to find a "Manage" or "Customize" option at the bottom.

### Step 3: Search for or Input the Token Contract Address
This is the most crucial step. You must correctly identify the token.
1.  **Search (Recommended for Major Tokens)**: In the search bar, type the token's **official name** (e.g., "Ethereum") or its **symbol** (e.g., "ETH"). Select it from the pre-loaded list. This is safe and straightforward.
2.  **Manual Add (For New/Unlisted Tokens)**: If the token doesn't appear in the search, you will need its **contract address**.
    *   **Warning**: Always obtain the contract address from the token's **official website, verified social media (like the project's Twitter), or reputable blockchain explorers (like Etherscan for Ethereum tokens). Never copy addresses from unverified sources.**
    *   Tap "Add Custom Token" or "Import by Contract Address".
    *   Select the correct **blockchain network** (e.g., Ethereum, BNB Smart Chain, Solana, etc.). The token exists on a specific network.
    *   Paste the verified contract address into the field. The token symbol and decimal places should auto-populate. Verify them against the official source.

### Step 4: Confirm and Add
Once the token details (Symbol, Name, Decimals) are filled and verified, click "Confirm", "Add", or "Import". The token will now appear in your wallet's asset list, showing a balance of 0 until you receive some.

### Step 5: Receiving the Token
To see a balance, you need to receive the token. Go to the token's page in your wallet, click "Receive", and share your wallet address (or a QR code) with the sender. Ensure the sender uses the same blockchain network you added the token for.

## Essential Pre-Checklist Before Adding Any Token (Security Must)

Before you paste any contract address, follow this safety protocol:

1.  **Verify the Contract Address**: Cross-check it on a block explorer. Look for the token's official website and match the address.
2.  **Check Token Verification**: On explorers like Etherscan, look for a blue checkmark or "Verified Contract" status. Be extremely cautious of unverified contracts.
3.  **Research the Token**: A quick search can reveal if the token is associated with "honeypot" scams (where you can buy but not sell) or other malicious activity.
4.  **Use a Test Transaction**: If dealing with a significant amount, consider sending a very small quantity first to ensure everything works correctly.

## Troubleshooting Common Issues (FAQ)

**Q: I added the token, but my balance is still zero or not showing.**
A: First, confirm the transaction was successful on a block explorer using your wallet address. If it was, try these steps within Bitget Wallet: 1) Pull down to refresh the asset list. 2) Toggle the token's visibility off and on in the "Manage Tokens" section. 3) Ensure you added the token on the correct blockchain network (e.g., you sent BEP-20 tokens but added the token on the Ethereum network).

**Q: Can I remove a token I've added?**
A: Yes. Go to "Manage Tokens" or your asset list, find the token, and there should be an option to "Hide" or "Remove". This only removes it from your view; it does not affect the tokens in your address on the blockchain.

**Q: Is it safe to add any token? What are the risks?**
A: The act of adding a token is safe—it simply allows your wallet interface to *see* tokens at your address. The **risk lies in interacting with malicious tokens**. Never approve transactions (like "Swap" or "Increase Allowance") for an untrusted token you've added, as it could drain your wallet. Adding is for viewing; interacting requires extreme caution.

**Q: I entered the wrong contract address. What should I do?**
A: Simply hide or remove the incorrectly added token as described above. Adding a wrong address only creates a useless entry in your wallet's list; it cannot steal funds unless you actively sign a transaction for it. Re-add the token with the correct address.
