## Understanding and Approving Wallet Authorization Prompts

A critical, yet often misunderstood, part of using decentralized features on OKX (like connecting to Web3 wallets or using the OKX Wallet) is the wallet authorization prompt. Here’s how to handle it safely:

* **What It Is**: When you connect an external wallet (e.g., MetaMask) or use OKX's built-in Web3 wallet to interact with a decentralized application (dApp), you'll see a prompt asking for authorization to "View Address" or "Spend" certain tokens.
* **Safety First**: **"View Address"** permissions are generally safe; they only allow the dApp to see your public wallet address. **"Spend" or "Approve"** permissions require extreme caution. They grant the dApp the ability to move specific tokens from your wallet, up to a limit you set.
* **Best Practice**: Always verify the dApp's authenticity. For "Spend" approvals, use the minimum necessary amount for your transaction instead of approving an unlimited amount. This simple habit, often overlooked, is a primary defense against potential smart contract exploits and can prevent significant, unexpected losses.
