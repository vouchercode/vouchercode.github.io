## Why Monitor Basis, Funding, and Open Interest on OKX?

For institutional participants, these three metrics form a foundational trinity for gauging market health beyond simple price action. Their interplay on a high-liquidity platform like OKX offers unparalleled insight:

*   **Basis as a Sentiment Gauge**: The difference between the perpetual futures price and the underlying spot index price. A persistent positive basis (contango) often signals bullish leverage demand, while a negative basis (backwardation) can indicate hedging pressure or bearish sentiment.
*   **Funding Rate as a Mechanism & Signal**: The periodic payments between long and short positions that tether the futures price to the spot index. Sustained positive funding rates suggest longs are paying shorts to maintain positions, often seen in strong uptrends. Conversely, negative funding can signal crowded short positions.
*   **Open Interest as a Commitment Metric**: The total number of outstanding derivative contracts. Rising OI alongside price suggests new money is fueling a trend, while rising OI during a price decline can signal aggressive new shorting. Declining OI often indicates trend exhaustion or unwinding.
