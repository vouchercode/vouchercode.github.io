## Step-by-Step Guide to Activating Hidden Features (Visual Walkthrough)

### Step 1: Access the Advanced Trading Interface
Log into your Binance account and navigate to the trading page for your desired pair (e.g., BTC/USDT). **Crucially**, do not use the default "Basic" view. Locate and click the switch or tab that changes the interface to "**Advanced**" or "**TradingView**" mode. This unlocks the full toolkit.
* **Note:** The layout may seem complex initially, but this is where strategic control is born.

### Step 2: Locate the Hidden Order Type Menu
In the order placement panel, you will typically see buttons for "Market" and "Limit." Next to these, there should be a small dropdown arrow or a text link that says "**More**" or "**Order Type**." Click this. This is the gateway. The menu that expands will reveal options like:
1.  **Limit** (Standard)
2.  **Market** (Standard)
3.  **Stop-Limit**
4.  **Post Only**
5.  **Immediate or Cancel (IOC)**

### Step 3: Implementing a "Post Only" Order
Select "**Post Only**" from the menu. When you place a limit order with this flag, the system will only execute it if it can be placed on the order book without immediately matching with an existing order (i.e., it will act as a maker). If it would execute as a taker, the order will be automatically cancelled. This is your key to guaranteed fee discounts.
* **Practical Use:** Place a buy order *slightly* below the current best bid or a sell order *slightly* above the best ask to increase the chance it posts to the book.

### Step 4: Configuring Slippage Tolerance (The Indirect Method)
While a direct retail slider doesn't exist, you can simulate it. For a buy order, set a **Limit** price at the maximum price you are willing to pay (your slippage tolerance). Then, in the same "Order Type" dropdown, select "**Immediate or Cancel (IOC)**". Enter your desired amount. The order will fill immediately at or below your limit price, and any unfilled portion vanishes. This combination effectively defines your slippage boundary.
