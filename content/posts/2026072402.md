## Why Focus on "Hidden Costs" in Dual-Currency Products?

Dual-Currency Investment products, which allow users to earn yields by committing to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a future date, are often marketed with attractive headline Annual Percentage Yields (APYs). However, the advertised rate is rarely the rate you keep. Several covert factors eat into your final return:

*   **Redemption/Penalty Fees:** Some platforms charge a significant fee if you need to redeem your principal before the settlement date, effectively negating any earned yield.
*   **Slippage & Execution Price:** The actual price at which your option is exercised (if it expires in-the-money) can differ from the expected price due to market volatility at settlement, impacting your final asset value.
*   **Opportunity Cost of Lock-up:** While your funds are locked, you miss out on other potential trading or staking opportunities. A slightly higher APY may not compensate for this lost flexibility.
*   **Liquidity & Availability:** The frequency of product issuance, minimum investment amounts, and total quota can limit your ability to deploy capital efficiently.

This test was designed to quantify these hidden costs under real market conditions in April 2026.
