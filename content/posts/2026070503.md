## Why Jurisdictional Changes Matter: Beyond Compliance Headaches

*   **Shift in Tax Treaty Networks:** The new jurisdiction's network of double taxation avoidance agreements (DTAAs) will apply. Gains or income reported through the exchange may be subject to withholding taxes or reporting requirements that differ drastically from the previous regime.
*   **Automatic Exchange of Information (AEOI):** The new jurisdiction's commitment to frameworks like the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) becomes paramount. HNWIs must assess whether their holdings will now be automatically reported to their home country's tax authority under a new, potentially more rigorous, data-sharing agreement.
*   **Definition of Taxable Events:** Jurisdictions vary in how they classify crypto transactions (e.g., as property, currency, or a unique asset class). A move could subtly change how staking rewards, airdrops, or even margin trading fees are treated for tax purposes, creating complex reconciliation needs.
