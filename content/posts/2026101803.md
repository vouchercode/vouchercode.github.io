## Your Step-by-Step Guide to Setting TP/SL on OKX (Visual Tutorial)

### Step 1: Access the Advanced Order Panel
Navigate to the trading pair you want to trade (e.g., BTC-USDT) on either Spot or Futures markets. Below the basic "Market" and "Limit" order tabs, locate and click the **"Trigger Order"** or **"Advanced"** order type. This is where TP/SL functionality resides.

### Step 2: Understand the Order Types
OKX offers several related order types. For beginners, focus on these two:
1.  **Stop-Loss Order (SL)**: An order to *sell* (for a long position) at a price *below* the current market price to limit losses. It becomes active only after the market price hits your specified "Trigger Price."
2.  **Take-Profit Order (TP)**: An order to *sell* (for a long position) at a price *above* the current market price to secure profits. It also activates once the "Trigger Price" is reached.

**Pro Tip**: Use the **"TP/SL" tab** which allows you to set both a profit target and a stop-loss level for an existing position simultaneously.

### Step 3: Configuring Your Parameters
1.  **For an Existing Position (Futures/Margin)**: Find your open position in the "Positions" tab. There should be a "TP/SL" button. Click it.
2.  **Set Trigger Price**: This is the market price at which your TP or SL order will be *placed* on the order book.
    *   **For SL**: Set this at a price level that defines your maximum acceptable loss.
    *   **For TP**: Set this at your desired profit target.
3.  **Set Order Price**: Once triggered, the order will be executed at this price. You can set it as "Market" for guaranteed fill (but potential slippage) or "Limit" for price control (but risk of non-fill).
4.  **Confirm and Submit**: Double-check all parameters. The system will show you the estimated P&L if the orders execute. Click "Confirm."

### Step 4: Monitor and Adjust
Once set, your TP/SL orders will appear in the "Open Orders" list as "Trigger Orders." You can modify or cancel them at any time before they are triggered. As the market moves, you may want to **trail your stop-loss** upward to protect increasing profits—a feature OKX supports.

## Beyond Basics: Advanced TP/SL Strategies for 2026

To truly future-proof your trading, move beyond static orders.

1.  **Trailing Stop-Loss**: Instead of a fixed price, set a stop-loss that follows the market price upward by a percentage or fixed amount. It automatically locks in profits while giving the trade room to grow. This is essential for capturing long-term trends expected in the next bull cycle.
2.  **OCO (One-Cancels-the-Other) Bracket Orders**: This is a powerful combo. You place a TP and an SL order simultaneously for the same position. When one order executes, the other is automatically canceled. It's the ultimate "set and forget" strategy for defining your risk-reward ratio upfront.
3.  **Partial TP/SL**: Don't close your entire position at once. Set a TP to sell 50% of your position at target X, and let the remainder run with a trailing stop, or set another TP at a higher level. This balances profit-taking with potential upside.

## Common Pitfalls & How to Avoid Them (FAQ)

**Q: I set a stop-loss, and it got triggered, then the price immediately went back up! (Stop-Hunting)**
A: This is frustrating but common in volatile markets. To mitigate: 1) Avoid placing SL orders at obvious round-number levels where many others cluster. 2) Use a wider stop-loss based on technical support/resistance levels, not an arbitrary small percentage. 3) Consider using a "Stop-Limit" order instead of "Stop-Market" to control the execution price, though it may not fill in a crash.

**Q: Is my money safe with these conditional orders? Does OKX guarantee execution?**
A: The orders are stored on OKX's secure servers. However, **execution is not guaranteed**. In cases of extreme volatility (flash crash/gap), a market order may fill at a worse price (slippage), and a limit order may not fill at all. TP/SL manages risk; it cannot eliminate all market risk.

**Q: Can I set TP/SL on the OKX mobile app?**
