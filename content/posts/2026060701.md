## Why Depth, Slippage, and TVL Are the Holy Trinity for Institutional Traders

For high-volume traders, the nominal trading fee is often just the tip of the iceberg. The true cost of execution is buried in **slippage**—the difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is actually executed. Slippage is directly governed by **market depth**, which represents the volume of buy and sell orders available at various price levels around the current market price. A deep order book can absorb large orders with minimal price impact. Furthermore, **Total Value Locked (TVL)** across OKX's native decentralized finance (DeFi) ecosystem, including its decentralized exchange (DEX) and Earn products, serves as a powerful indicator of capital commitment, platform health, and the underlying liquidity reservoir that ultimately supports spot and derivatives markets. Analyzing these three metrics in concert provides a complete picture of trading efficiency and platform robustness.
