## Why is On-Chain Data the Ultimate Arbiter of Exchange Safety?

Traditional financial audits have their place, but in the transparent world of blockchain, on-chain data provides a real-time, immutable ledger of an exchange's financial health. For institutions, this data is not just informative—it's foundational for risk assessment.

*   **Transparency & Verifiability**: Every transaction involving exchange wallets is recorded on public blockchains like Bitcoin and Ethereum. This allows for independent verification of asset reserves, a concept central to the "Proof of Reserves" framework.
*   **Liquidity Analysis**: By tracking inflows and outflows from known exchange wallets, analysts can gauge user sentiment (net deposits vs. withdrawals) and the platform's ability to handle large-scale redemptions without slippage.
*   **Early Warning Signals**: Unusual movements of large sums, such as transfers to unknown wallets or sudden spikes in stablecoin reserves, can serve as leading indicators of potential stress or strategic shifts.
