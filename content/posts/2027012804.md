## The Hidden Cost Framework: Beyond the Fee Schedule

To manage these costs, one must first map them. Our internal reference model decomposes total execution cost into visible and hidden components.

### Component 1: Funding Rate Mismatch & Prediction Slippage
This is often the largest hidden drain. The cost isn't just the paid/received funding rate; it's the **mismatch between predicted and actual rates** when rolling positions.
*   **The Model's Role**: Actuarial models analyze historical rate sequences, volatility regimes, and open interest trends to forecast funding rate probabilities, optimizing the timing of position entry/rollover to minimize expected cost.

### Component 2: Slippage on Large Orders
Even on a liquid platform like OKX, market impact exists. Hidden cost arises from the difference between the expected mid-price fill and the actual executed price, exacerbated during volatile spells.
*   **The Model's Role**: Models simulate order book depth and likely market impact for different order sizes and slice strategies, providing an "expected slippage" cost to be factored into trade feasibility.

### Component 3: Opportunity Cost of Collateral
Margin posted for contracts (especially in cross-margin mode) has an alternative use cost—it could be earning yield elsewhere in DeFi or in spot holdings.
*   **The Model's Role**: By calculating the risk-adjusted yield foregone on locked collateral and optimizing margin efficiency (e.g., switching between isolated/cross, using portfolio margin features), the model quantifies and helps minimize this drag.

### Component 4: Gas & Network Fees for On-Chain Operations
For institutions moving funds between CeFi and DeFi or executing on-chain settlements, Ethereum gas fees or other network costs are a variable but significant factor.
*   **The Model's Role**: While not controllable by OKX, models can optimize withdrawal timing and batch transactions based on historical gas price patterns, reducing this external cost burden.
