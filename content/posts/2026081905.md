## Why Monitor Basis, Funding Rate, and Open Interest for Arbitrage?

For the quantitative trader, these three data points are not just statistics; they are the fundamental signals that reveal market inefficiencies ripe for exploitation. Their interplay defines the risk-reward profile of carry trades, basis trades, and funding rate arbitrage.

*   **Basis (Spot-Futures Spread)**: The price difference between a cryptocurrency's spot price and its futures contract price. A positive basis (futures trading at a premium) suggests bullish sentiment and can facilitate cash-and-carry arbitrage.
*   **Funding Rate**: A periodic payment exchanged between long and short position holders in perpetual swap contracts to tether the contract price to the spot price. A persistently high positive or negative rate creates opportunities for funding rate capture strategies.
*   **Open Interest (OI)**: The total number of outstanding derivative contracts that have not been settled. Rising OI often indicates new money entering the market and can signal the strength or sustainability of a trend, impacting the stability of arbitrage setups.
