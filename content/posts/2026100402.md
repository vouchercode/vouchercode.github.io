## Why is the "Real Fee Rate" the Ultimate Decisive Factor?

Many exchanges advertise "low fees" or "zero fees for makers," but the actual cost borne by users often includes multiple hidden components. The "real fee rate" we refer to is the total cost incurred from depositing funds to finally withdrawing profits, which mainly includes:

*   **Trading Fees**: The most direct cost, usually composed of maker and taker fees, often discounted by holding platform tokens or based on trading volume.
*   **Withdrawal Fees**: Fees charged for transferring assets out of the exchange. This is a fixed cost that is often overlooked.
*   **Spread Costs**: The difference between the buy and sell prices on the order book. On platforms with poor liquidity, this implicit cost can be significant.
*   **Funding Rate (for derivatives)**: For perpetual contract traders, this is a periodic cost or income that directly affects profitability.

Simply comparing nominal trading fees is meaningless. A true fee showdown must simulate real user behavior.
