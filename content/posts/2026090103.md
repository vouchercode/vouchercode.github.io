## Why Slippage, Funding, and TVL Are the Institutional Trinity

While retail traders often focus on spot prices, professional desks model their P&L around three interlocking pillars: execution quality, cost of carry, and systemic liquidity resilience.

*   **Slippage as Execution Leakage**: Slippage represents the difference between the expected price of a trade and the price at which it is actually executed. In volatile or shallow markets, this can erode profits significantly. For large orders, it's not a cost but a *certainty* that must be managed.
*   **Funding Rate as a Strategic Cost**: In perpetual swap markets, the funding rate is a periodic payment between long and short positions to tether the contract price to the spot index. A consistently positive or negative rate can turn a neutral position into a loss-making one over time, making it a critical factor in holding periods and basis trading.
*   **TVL Depth as the Ultimate Safety Net**: Total Value Locked across an exchange's smart contracts, staking pools, and DeFi integrations is a proxy for its capital gravity. High TVL suggests robust liquidity, which directly correlates with lower slippage and more stable funding rates, especially during market stress.
